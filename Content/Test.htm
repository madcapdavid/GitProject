Understanding the Miscellaneous Registration Options

The Miscellaneous Registration options are unique registration options for limited-time registration or additional documentation that requires different handling than regular registration.

From the Customer > Miscellaneous Registration menu on the main RTS POS page, you can issue and manage:

  • Timed Permits for temporary registration that allows vehicles to travel along roadways for a specific period (72 hour, 144 hour, 30 day), for a specific delivery purpose of goods only, and for a single trip that originates in, routes through, or ends in a Texas city
  • Disabled Parking Placards for people with permanent and temporary disabilities
  • Temporary Additional Weigh Permits for hauling a larger tonnage than the registered gross weight of the vehicle over a limited period of less than one year
  • Non-Resident Agriculture Permits for out-of-state vehicles hauling agricultural products and machinery used to harvest the agricultural products within Texas
  • Tow Truck License Plates for vehicles to tow, winch, or otherwise move disabled motor vehicles along roadways
    Note: Small tow truck plates are processed at the Regional Service Centers and HQ only.

Understanding Timed Permits

You can issue several different types of timed permits to allow vehicles to travel on roadways in Texas in lieu of regular registration or because of certain temporary conditions, such as:

  • 72 Hour or 144 Hour permits for laden trucks, truck-tractors, trailers, semitrailers, or motor buses. You can also issue these permits to Texas residents for temporary movement of commercial vehicles with a heavier gross weight than originally registered as well as farm or soil conservation vehicles operating at their maximum legal weight but for a use other than their original purpose.
  • One Trip permits for unladen vehicles to move along a route between an originating city and destination city within 15 days from the effective date of the permit. These permits can be issued to passenger cars, motorcycles, charter buses and private buses from another state, and Park Model Trailers less than 400 square feet in size with or without passengers and property, but commercial vehicles and truck/truck tractors must be empty.
  • 30 Day permits for passenger vehicles, motorcycles, private buses, trailers, and semitrailers not exceeding 10,000 pounds gross weight, and light commercial vehicles with less than one ton carrying capacity. Commercial vehicles exceeding a rating of one ton are eligible for the permit if operating unladen. Proof of insurance is required for this permit.
  • Factory Delivery permits to allow for laden vehicles to deliver products to a specific destination in the State of Texas.

In addition, you can run inquiries on the permits and issue duplicate replacement receipts for them.

Issuing 72-Hour or 144-Hour Timed Permits

These two permits are issued with a specific date and time when the permit takes effect and the system calculates the date/time they expire.

Issuing a 72-hour or 144-hour permit involves:

  • Locating the vehicle and selecting the permit type.
  • Entering information about the vehicle and its owner as well as the expiration date and time of the permit.
  • Working with the permit inventory to issue the permit.
  • Working with the fees due for the transaction.
  • Processing the permit transaction and payment.

Top of Page

Locating the Vehicle and Selecting the Permit Type

If you know the vehicle identification number of the vehicle for which the applicant wants the permit, you can enter it and the information will be automatically filled in for you on the appropriate page. Otherwise, you can select No VIN and enter the information manually.

  • On the main page action bar, click Customer > Miscellaneous Registration > Timed Permit > Permit Application.
  • On the Timed Permit VIN Key Selection MRG007 page:

    1. Type the 17 number-letter string into the VIN field or click the No VIN checkbox.
    2. Click the Enter button.
  • On the Permit Search Results INQ004 page, select the appropriate record and click the Enter button.

  • From the Timed Permit Types MRG005 page, click the appropriate 72-Hour or 144-Hour option.

  • Continue with the section about entering permit information.

    • Top of Page

    Entering Permit Information

    If you have successfully entered a VIN, the Vehicle Info fields are automatically filled in from the vehicle record and only the Name and Address of the vehicle owner is required.

    Once you enter the date and time when the 72-hour or 144 hour period for the permit will begin, the system automatically calculates the date and time when the permit expires.

  • On the Timed Permit MRG006 page, if the Vehicle Info is not already filled in:

    1. For Year/Make, type in the year the vehicle was manufactured in the first field and the 4-letter identifier of the manufacturer in the second field
    2. For Body Style, type in the two or four letter designation.
    3. For VIN, type in the 17 numbers and letters of the vehicle identification number.
    4. For Effective Date, type in the month, day and year (MM/DD/YYYY) when the period should begin.
    5. For Effective Time, type in the hour and minutes (such as 09:00) when the period should begin and click AM or PM from the drop-down list as appropriate.
      Note: The Expiration Date and Time are calculated and filled in automatically.
  • For Applicant Type, if the applicant is a:
    • Person:
      1. Be sure the Individual option is selected.
      2. In the Individual Name area, type in the First Name and Last Name of the person applying for the permit. (The Middle Initial is optional.)
      3. For Address:
        1. In the first field, type in the number and name of the street of the applicant’s residence.
        2. In the second field, type in any additional street information (like apartment number or suite) for the address.
          Note: For long addresses, logically divide the information between the two fields.
        3. In the third field, type in the name of the city that corresponds to the applicant’s residence.
        4. In the fourth field, if the address is not in TX, type in the 2-letter abbreviation for the state that corresponds to the address of the applicant (or the 4-letter code if not in the US).
        5. In the fifth field, type in the 5 numbers for the ZIP code string.
        6. In the sixth field, optionally type in the 4 numbers of the extended ZIP code.
      4. For E-mail, enter the email address where the applicant can be contacted.
      5. For Phone No., enter the three-number area code followed by the 7 numbers of the telephone number where the applicant can be reached.
      6. Click the Enter button.
    • Business entity:
      1. Click the Business option.
      2. In the Business Name area, type in name of the business, firm, corporation, and so on.
      3. For Address:
        1. In the first field, type in the number and name of the street of the applicant’s residence.
        2. In the second field, type in any additional street information (like apartment number or suite) for the address.
          Note: For long addresses, logically divide the information between the two fields.
        3. In the third field, type in the name of the city that corresponds to the applicant’s residence.
        4. In the fourth field, if the address is not in TX, type in the 2-letter abbreviation for the state that corresponds to the address of the applicant (or the 4-letter code if not in the US).
        5. In the fifth field, type in the 5 numbers for the ZIP code string.
        6. In the sixth field, optionally type in the 4 numbers of the extended ZIP code.
      4. For E-mail, enter the email address where the applicant can be contacted.
      5. For Phone No., enter the three-number area code followed by the 7 numbers of the telephone number where the applicant can be reached.
      6. Click the Enter button.
    • Continue with the section about working with inventory.

      Working with Permit Inventory

      If permit inventory has not been allocated to the workstation, you will need to input the permit number.

      Note: If inventory is automatically assigned, this page does not display. Skip to the next section about the fees due.

    • On the Inventory - Item Number Input INV001 page, type in the permit number and click the Enter button.

    • If the Inventory-Item Number Not Found INV029 page displays, click the Enter button.

      • Is correct, click the Enter button.

      • Top of Page

      Working with the Fees Due for the Transaction

      In addition to verifying any fees being charged for this transaction, you can add a miscellaneous fee if appropriate before payment is collected as well as add donations to the State Parks’ and Veterans’ funds upon customer request.

      If you need to make a change to information involved with a transaction, you can return to a previous page from the Fees Due page to modify the information without cancelling the transaction.

    • On the Fees Due PMT004 page, verify that the information is correct and if it:

      • Is correct as is (no additional fees will be applied), click the Enter button and continue to the section about processing the transaction.
      • Contains an amount that needs to be credited back or, refunded to the customer:
        1. Click the Credit button.
        2. On the Credit PMT003 popup, type the amount and click the OK button.

      • Does not reflect miscellaneous fees accrued:
        1. Click the Misc Fees button.
        2. On the Misc Fee PMT002 popup, type the amount in the field and click the OK button.

        3. Back on the Fees Due PMT004 page, verify that the fees have been added.
    • If the customer indicates they want to add a donation to the:
      • State Parks Fund:
        1. Click the State Parks button.
        2. On the State Parks Fund PMT002 popup, type the amount in the field and click the OK button.

        3. Back on the Fees Due PMT004 page, verify that the fees have been added.
      • Veterans’ Fund:
        1. Click the Veterans’ Fund button.
        2. On the Veterans’ Fund PMT002 popup, type the amount in the field and click the OK button.

        3. Back on the Fees Due PMT004 page, verify that the fees have been added.
    • When you are finished, click the Enter button and continue with the section about processing the transaction.

      • Top of Page

      Processing the Transaction

      When you are finished entering information and the Transaction Pending page displays, you can:

      • Continue on and collect payment to close out the transaction.
      • Set the transaction aside to retain all of the information collected without collecting payment at this time (referred to as Pending). This is useful when a customer has multiple transaction requests.

      If the amount of fees display as $0.00, you can set aside the transaction to perform other transactions for the same customer.

      When processing a transaction where no fees are accrued (that is the amount displays as $0.00), clicking the Enter button on the Transaction page sends the receipt to print and displays the RTS POS main menu again without displaying the payment collection pages.

      Optionally Placing the Transaction in the Pending Mode

      If the customer needs to perform other transactions, you can place the current transaction in a pending mode by setting it aside and recalling it later. The customer name displays on the Set Aside page and you can accept that name or edit it to make it clearer for recalling.

      1. On the Pending Transactions page, verify the information and then click the Set Aside button.

      2. On the Set Aside CUS002 page, click the Enter button.

      Note: You can add more detail or edit the name to distinguish it from other names that may display in the list of set aside transactions before clicking the Enter button.

      Top of Page

      Collecting Payment Due

      You can accept several types of payment, including cash, checks, credit, card or ACH (which is an account clearing house, such as an electronic bank account withdrawal).

      If the transaction or cash register receipt does not print correctly or is damaged, you can produce another receipt from the main menu by selecting Miscellaneous > Reprint Receipt.

      1. On the Pending Transactions page, verify the information and then click the Enter button.
      2. On the Payment PMT001 page:

        1. For Payment Code, click the appropriate option from the drop-down list.
        2. For Amount, type in the monetary amount of this transaction.
        3. If CHECK is selected (as the Payment Code), type the number displayed on the check in the Check No field.
        4. If a Change Due amount is displayed, for Change Type, click the:
          • CASH option to pay the Change Due to customer out of the cash drawer.
          • CHECK option if they will receive a check for the amount.
        5. Click the Enter button.
      3. On the Confirm Action CTL001 page that asks if you want to complete the transaction, click the Yes button to complete the transaction and print the receipt.

            Managing Disabled Parking Placards and Plates

            Temporary and permanent parking placards can be issued for people with disabilities (mobility, visual, and so on) and to institutions (such residential communities for retirement, veterans, and disabled persons) that use registered vans or buses to transport their residents.

            A placard applicant must provide notarized written proof of the disability or have a doctor-issued prescription. The written proof or prescription must indicate whether the disability is permanent or temporary.

            Blue placards are issued for a permanent disability and are valid for a maximum of four years. They are issued at no charge, but a $2 fee is charged for replacements. The applicant can be issued two blue placards or one blue placard and a set of disabled license plates.

            Red placards are issued for a temporary disability and are valid for six months from the date of issuance, or until the end of the disability, whichever comes first. A $5 fee is charged for this type of placard. The applicant can be issued two red placards. Temporary disabilities do not qualify for disabled license plates.

            Records are created and maintained for each placard applicant. You can view the history of all active placards and those no longer in use (for example, the placard expired, was stolen, or was revoked) in the record as well.

            You can:

            • Issue a placard to a disabled person or issue placards to a facility
            • Add an additional placard to a disabled person or facility
            • Replace a lost, stolen, or damaged placard
            • Delete a placard that has been lost, stolen, or damaged
            • Renew a placard before it expires
            • Reinstate a revoked placard

            Managing Non-Resident Agriculture Permits

            You can issue 30-day temporary registration permits to vehicles not registered in Texas to move:

            • Farm and other agricultural products from their place of production in Texas to market, storage, or railhead within 75 miles from the place of production
            • Machinery used to harvest these commodities (such as combines and implements of husbandry)
            • Farm products produced outside of Texas but marketed or processed in Texas or moved to points in Texas for shipment

            The permit is issued for the sufficient weight to transport the heaviest gross load to be transported during the permit's validity by the nonresident. Each vehicle in a combination must be issued a separate permit.

            The permit fee is based on the gross weight of the vehicle and calculated by taking 1/12th of the applicable annual registration fee. The 11% diesel fee must be collected for trucks or truck-tractors with a manufacturer's rated carrying capacity exceeding two tons.

            A Temporary Non-Resident Registration Permit is valid for a 30-day period inclusive of the date of issuance and cannot be issued to the non-resident more than 3 times during a single vehicle registration year. After the third permit, the non-resident must register the vehicle with regular Texas license plates or a temporary 72/144 Hour Permit. The vehicles cannot qualify for Farm Truck license plates.

            You can also run an inquiry about existing permits and issue duplicate receipts.
